The Problem

Large brands have data teams. You don't.

Chanel knows which aesthetics are rising six months before the market does. Prada has consumer psychologists mapping purchase intent. Independent designers and small labels make the same decisions — what to design, when to launch, who to collaborate with — but without any of that infrastructure.

Volna changes that. It tracks 65+ luxury brands across Google Trends and Pinterest, identifies the aesthetic clusters that are gaining momentum, and translates that signal into specific, actionable intelligence for independent creatives.

Modules

Six tools built for independent creatives

01

Consumer Intelligence Report

A complete consumer profile for the audience most likely to discover and buy from brands in your aesthetic cluster. Demographics, psychographics, purchase triggers, platform behavior, and cultural influences — grounded in academic research and real signal data.

02

Aesthetic Positioning Map

UMAP clustering places 65+ luxury brands in 2D space based on their behavioral fingerprint across Google and Pinterest. See exactly where your aesthetic sits in the current market, which clusters are rising, and where the white space is for an independent brand to gain traction.

03

Cross-Platform Signal Analysis

Google Trends captures search intent. Pinterest captures aspirational saving. When they agree, the conviction is strong. When they diverge, there is a gap between what consumers dream about and what they pursue — a gap that represents a strategic opportunity for independent designers.

04

Collaboration Finder

Enter your brand name and receive a ranked shortlist of luxury brands whose consumer base most behaviorally overlaps with yours. Each match comes with a five-part collaboration brief covering shared audience, what each brand brings, optimal format, timing, and how to initiate outreach.

05

Launch Timing Engine

Uses seasonal peak analysis, 6-month Holt-Winters forecasts, and the Google-leads-Pinterest lag to calculate your optimal launch window, announcement date, production deadline, and Pinterest content timing — with a personalised calendar built from your aesthetic cluster's data.

06

Luxury Market Context

Track 65+ luxury brands across four categories — Established Luxury, French and Italian Houses, American and British Designers, and Avant-Garde and Emerging — as the macro signal backdrop that sets the aesthetic agenda your independent brand operates within.

Methodology

How Volna Works

01

Data Collection

Weekly trend data fetched from Google Trends and Pinterest across 65+ luxury brands, capturing both search intent and aspirational consumer behavior in the US market.

02

Forecasting

Holt-Winters exponential smoothing projects each brand's interest trajectory 6 months forward, accounting for long-run momentum and annual seasonal patterns.

03

UMAP Clustering

Dimensionality reduction maps all brands into 2D aesthetic clusters based on their combined Google and Pinterest behavioral fingerprint — revealing the hidden structure of the luxury market.

04

Cross-Validation

Google and Pinterest signals are correlated and lead/lag analyzed to identify the aspiration-intent gap — where consumers dream more than they search, and vice versa.

05

AI Intelligence

Groq AI synthesizes all data into consumer profiles, cultural analyses, collaboration briefs, launch calendars, and positioning strategies — cited, specific, and grounded in real signal data.

Data Sources

Built on Real Signal Data

Google Trends

Weekly aggregate search interest for luxury brand keywords in the US market via pytrends. Captures active consumer research and purchase intent — what people are actively looking for.

Pinterest Trends API

Weekly aggregate pin and save counts per brand keyword. Captures aspirational consumer behavior — what people are saving, mood boarding, and planning to incorporate into their wardrobe.

Groq AI

AI-generated intelligence using Llama 3.3 70B, grounded in consumer psychology research, cultural theory, and cited academic sources — translating raw data into specific, actionable designer guidance.

About the Project

An independent data science project.

Volna is developed independently to demonstrate applied machine learning, time series forecasting, UMAP dimensionality reduction, and AI-powered market intelligence in the context of independent fashion.

The platform uses Google Trends, the Pinterest Trends API, and the Groq AI API to collect, analyze, and interpret brand interest data. No personal user data is collected or stored at any point. All analysis is performed on aggregate, anonymized trend signals only.
