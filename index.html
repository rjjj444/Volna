The Problem

Large brands have data teams. You don't.

Chanel knows which aesthetics are gaining momentum six months before the market does. Prada has researchers mapping consumer aspiration across platforms. Independent designers, stylists, and emerging labels make the same decisions — what to design, when to launch, which direction to lean into — but without any of that infrastructure.

Volna changes that. It tracks 65+ luxury brands across Google Trends, Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram, Substack, and YouTube, identifies the aesthetic clusters that are gaining cultural momentum, and translates those signals into specific, actionable intelligence for independent creatives.

Modules

Tools built for independent creatives

01

Aesthetic Positioning Map

UMAP clustering places 65+ luxury brands in 2D space based on their behavioral fingerprint across Google and Pinterest. See exactly where your aesthetic sits in the current market, which clusters are gaining momentum, and where the genuine white space is for an independent voice.

02

Cross-Platform Signal Analysis

Google Trends captures search intent. Pinterest captures aspirational saving. TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Substack capture cultural conversation. When all six agree, the conviction is strong. When they diverge, there is a gap between aspiration and pursuit — a gap that independent designers can move into.

03

Aesthetic Discovery Search

Search any keyword — a brand name, aesthetic term, material, silhouette, color, or cultural reference — and see what the full cross-platform data returns. Live suggestions as you type, filters by momentum signal and platform, and saved searches so you can track what matters to you over time.

04

Personalised Intelligence Report

Answer seven questions about your aesthetic sensibility and receive a personalised report showing which luxury brands you're behaviorally adjacent to, which platforms are most active in your aesthetic zone, and what the timing signals say about when and where to focus your creative energy.

05

Launch Timing Engine

Uses seasonal peak analysis, 6-month Holt-Winters forecasts, and the Google-leads-Pinterest lag to calculate your optimal launch window, announcement date, and platform content timing — with a personalised calendar built from your aesthetic cluster's cross-platform data.

06

Luxury Market Context

Track 65+ luxury brands across four categories — Established Luxury, French and Italian Houses, American and British Designers, and Avant-Garde and Emerging — as the macro signal backdrop that sets the aesthetic agenda your independent work operates within.

Methodology

How Volna Works

01

Data Collection

Trend data collected across six platforms — Google Trends, Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram, Substack, and YouTube — capturing search intent, aspirational saving, social engagement, and cultural discourse simultaneously.

02

Forecasting

Holt-Winters exponential smoothing projects each brand's interest trajectory 6 months forward, accounting for long-run momentum and annual seasonal patterns in both Google and Pinterest data.

03

UMAP Clustering

Dimensionality reduction maps all brands into 2D aesthetic clusters based on their combined behavioral fingerprint across platforms — revealing the hidden structure of the luxury landscape.

04

Cross-Validation

Signals from all six platforms are correlated and weighted to identify aspiration-intent gaps — where cultural conversation is outpacing active search, and where search has outrun cultural resonance.

05

AI Intelligence

Groq AI synthesizes all cross-platform data into personalised aesthetic reports, cultural analyses, launch timing briefs, and positioning strategies — specific, cited, and grounded in real signal data.

Data Sources

Six platforms. One picture.

Google Trends

Weekly aggregate search interest for luxury brand and aesthetic keywords in the US market. Captures active consumer research and purchase intent — what people are actively pursuing.

Pinterest

Weekly aggregate pin and save counts per brand keyword. Captures aspirational consumer behavior — what people are mood boarding, planning, and aspiring toward before they search.

TikTok

Creator engagement, hashtag co-occurrence, and video performance data via Sociavault. Captures real-time cultural momentum — the aesthetic conversations happening right now.

Instagram

Post, Reel, and hashtag engagement data via Sociavault. Saves on Instagram are the strongest purchase intent signal on any social platform — a direct read on commercial aspiration.

Substack

Fashion newsletter engagement, topic co-occurrence, and restack networks via Substack's public API. Captures the intellectual discourse shaping how fashion's most engaged audiences think.

YouTube

Channel and video performance data via Sociavault. Captures long-form fashion engagement — documentary culture, critique, and the deeper aesthetic conversations that drive sustained interest.

About the Project

An independent data science project.

Volna is developed independently to demonstrate applied machine learning, time series forecasting, UMAP dimensionality reduction, and AI-powered market intelligence in the context of independent fashion.

The platform collects publicly available trend data across six platforms — Google Trends, Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram, Substack, and YouTube — and translates that signal into personalised intelligence for designers and creative professionals. Account data is stored securely via Supabase. No personal browsing or usage data is collected. All trend analysis is performed on aggregate, anonymised signals only.
