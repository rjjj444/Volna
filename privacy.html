Privacy Notice

Volna Last updated: March 2026 hello@volnatrends.com

01

Introduction

Volna ("we", "us", "our") is a fashion trend analytics platform developed and operated by a single independent developer. Volna provides market intelligence, consumer profiling, collaboration matching, and launch timing tools for independent designers, small fashion brands, and emerging labels.

This privacy notice explains how Volna collects, uses, and protects information when you use our platform. We are committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and applicable US privacy laws.

Questions about this notice: hello@volnatrends.com

02

What Data We Collect

Volna is designed to minimize personal data collection. The platform operates primarily on aggregate, anonymized trend data from third-party sources across 65+ luxury fashion brands.

Data we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email address, or contact details (unless you contact us directly)
  • Your location data
  • Your browsing history or behavioral data
  • Any Pinterest user data, personal boards, or private content
  • Any Google account data or personal search history
  • Any data about the independent brands or designers who use the platform

Data we may collect depending on final app design

  • Account registration details (name and email) if user accounts are implemented
  • Custom brand names entered into the Collaboration Finder or Launch Timing Engine — used only to fetch publicly available Google Trends and Pinterest data for that brand name, not stored permanently
  • Usage data such as which features you interact with, collected in aggregate and anonymized form
  • Technical data such as browser type, device type, and IP address for security and performance monitoring

Third-party data sources we query

  • Google Trends (via pytrends): Aggregate, anonymized search interest data for fashion brand and aesthetic trend keywords. No personal Google user data is accessed.
  • Pinterest Trends API: Aggregate, anonymized weekly pin and save count data for fashion brand keywords in the US market. No Pinterest user data, personal boards, private content, or individual user information is accessed at any point.
  • Groq AI API: Aggregated, anonymized trend data is sent to the Groq API to generate consumer intelligence reports, collaboration recommendations, and market analysis. No personal user data is included in these requests.

03

How We Use Data

Any data collected is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To provide trend analysis, forecasting, and market intelligence functionality within the Volna platform
  • To generate AI-powered consumer profiles, collaboration recommendations, launch timing briefs, and positioning strategies using aggregated trend signals
  • To power the Custom Brand Analysis feature — fetching publicly available search data for brand names entered by users
  • To improve and maintain the performance of the platform
  • To respond to enquiries if you contact us directly

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose beyond operating the platform.

04

Legal Basis for Processing

For users in the European Union and United Kingdom, we process data on the following legal bases under Article 6 of the GDPR:

Article 6(1)(f)Legitimate interests — Processing aggregate trend data to provide the core analytical and intelligence functionality of the platform
Article 6(1)(b)Contract performance — If you create an account, processing your registration details to provide you access to the platform
Article 6(1)(a)Consent — For any optional data collection beyond what is strictly necessary to operate the platform

05

Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell your personal data to any third party under any circumstances.

We share data with the following third-party services solely to operate the platform:

  • Google (pytrends): Aggregate keyword trend queries only. Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Pinterest: Aggregate keyword trend queries via the Pinterest Trends API. Pinterest's privacy policy: policy.pinterest.com/privacy-policy
  • Groq: Aggregated, anonymized trend data for AI-generated analysis and intelligence reports. No personal user data included. Groq's privacy policy: groq.com/privacy-policy

No personal data is transferred to these third parties as part of normal platform operation.

06

International Data Transfers

Volna operates from the United States. If you are accessing the platform from the European Union or United Kingdom, please be aware that data may be processed in countries outside the EEA or UK, including the United States, where our third-party API providers operate.

Where such transfers occur, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with GDPR Chapter V, including reliance on Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions where applicable.

07

Data Retention

  • Aggregate trend data fetched from Google Trends and Pinterest is stored locally in CSV format for analysis purposes and is not linked to any individual user
  • Custom brand names entered into the platform are used only for the duration of a single analysis session and are not stored permanently
  • If user accounts are implemented, account data will be retained for as long as your account remains active, and deleted within 30 days of account closure upon request
  • If you contact us directly, any correspondence will be retained for up to 12 months and then securely deleted

09

Cookies and Tracking

At present Volna does not use cookies or tracking technologies beyond what is strictly necessary to operate the platform. If this changes, this privacy notice will be updated and users will be informed.

10

Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect any data processed by the platform, including secure storage of API credentials as environment variables rather than hardcoded values, limiting data collection to only what is necessary for the platform to function, and not storing personal data beyond what is required for the active analysis session.

11
